Soils and Tree Health

Soil Analysis

Review the major parts of a soil-analysis report and how they inform tree-care decisions.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the major components of a soil-analysis report.
  • Interpret soil pH results.
  • Recognize the importance of soil texture and organic matter.
  • Evaluate reported nutrient levels.
  • Use soil-test information to support tree-care decisions.

Major Soil Characteristics

Soil pH

Describes how acidic or alkaline the soil is and affects nutrient availability and biological activity.

Soil Texture

Describes the relative amounts of sand, silt, and clay in a soil sample.

Organic Matter

Can improve soil structure, water retention, nutrient-holding capacity, and biological activity.

Nutrient Levels

Reports may include phosphorus, potassium, calcium, magnesium, and other essential elements.

Soluble Salts

High levels can interfere with water uptake and may damage tree roots.

Cation Exchange Capacity

CEC indicates a soil's ability to hold positively charged nutrient ions.

Important Reminder

Soil-test numbers should not be interpreted in isolation. Consider the tree species, observed symptoms, site history, soil conditions, root environment, and the testing laboratory's reference ranges.
