Tree Nutrition and Fertilization

Complete Fertilizer Practice Questions

Located on the front of a bag of complete fertilizer, you will find the guaranteed analysis, often in the format X-Y-Z (e.g., 30-12-8). These values indicate the percentage of each of N-P-K.

This activity helps you to practice the math used in determining fertilizer application amounts.

How to Find the Amount of Each Nutrient

In a complete fertilizer, the three primary nutrients are nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K).

The N-P-K label, also called the guaranteed analysis, shows the percentage of nutrients in the product by weight.

On the label, nitrogen is listed as actual N, but phosphorus and potassium are usually reported as P2O5 and K2O.

This means the label tells us how much of each labeled nutrient is in the fertilizer, not the exact elemental amount of phosphorus or potassium.

For a guaranteed analysis of 30-15-10, that means 30% N, 15% P2O5, and 10% K2O.

We can calculate how many pounds of each nutrient are in the bag by multiplying the total bag weight by each percentage and dividing by 100.

Nutrient Weight = Total Bag Weight × Nutrient Percentage ÷ 100

Worked Example

A 20 lb bag of complete fertilizer with a guaranteed analysis of 10–6–3 contains 10% nitrogen, 6% phosphorus, and 3% potassium. The amount of each nutrient in the bag is calculated as follows:

  • Nitrogen: 20 × 10 ÷ 100 = 2.0 lb
  • Available phosphate: 20 × 6 ÷ 100 = 1.2 lb
  • Soluble potash: 20 × 3 ÷ 100 = 0.6 lb

In other words, the numbers on the bag tell you the percentage of each nutrient by mass, which can be converted into the actual weight of nutrient in the product.

You can also simplify the calculation by using the decimal form of the percentage instead of dividing by 100. Since 10% is the same as 0.10, you can multiply the bag weight directly by 0.10 to get the nutrient mass. This is a faster way to do the same calculation.

Worked Example

A 10 lb bag of complete fertilizer with a guaranteed analysis of 20-10-5 contains the following amounts of nutrients:

  • Nitrogen: 10 × 0.20 = 2.0 lb
  • Available phosphate: 10 × 0.10 = 1.0 lb
  • Soluble potash: 10 × 0.05 = 0.5 lb
