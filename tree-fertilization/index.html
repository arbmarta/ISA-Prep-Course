Tree Nutrition and Fertilization

Complete Fertilizer Practice Questions

A bag of complete fertilizer - that is, a fertilizer that contains nitrogen (N), phosphate (P), and potassium (K) - includes a guaranteed analysis on the front of the bag. This guaranteed analysis identifies the percentage of each of N, P, and K. When applying fertilizer, it is important to correspond the amount of fertilizer to the required amount of nitrogen, phosphate, and potassium. This activity helps you to practice the math used in determining fertilizer application amounts.

How to calculate each answer

Nutrient mass = bag mass × nutrient percentage ÷ 100

Worked example

A 20 kg bag of 10–6–3 fertilizer contains:

  • Nitrogen: 20 × 10 ÷ 100 = 2.0 kg
  • Available phosphate: 20 × 6 ÷ 100 = 1.2 kg
  • Soluble potash: 20 × 3 ÷ 100 = 0.6 kg
