Tree Nutrition and Fertilization

Complete Fertilizer Practice Questions

Located on the front of a bag of complete fertilizer, you will find the guaranteed analysis, often in the format X-Y-Z (e.g., 30-12-8). These values indicate the percentage of each of N-P-K. This activity helps you to practice the math used in determining fertilizer application amounts.

How to Find the Amount of Each Nutrient

In a complete fertilizer, there are three nutrients: nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K). A complete fertilizer includes some amount of each of these three nutrients, and the percentage of each is provided in the N-P-K label, or the "guaranteed analysis". We are often interested in the amount of a given nutrient for a given bag of fertilizer.

First, we need to identify the percentage of each of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. For a guaranteed analysis of 30-15-10, this would be 30% nitrogen, 15% phosphorus, and 10% potassium.

Second, to find the amount of each nutrient in the bag, multiply the total bag weight by the nutrient percentage, then divide by 100.

Nutrient Weight = Total Bag Weight × Nutrient Percentage ÷ 100

Worked Example

A 20 kg bag of complete fertilizer with a guaranteed analysis of 10–6–3 would contain 10% nitrogen, 6% phosphorous, and 3% potassium. The weight of each nutrient inside the bag is calculated as follows:

  • Nitrogen: 20 × 10 ÷ 100 = 2.0 kg
  • Available phosphate: 20 × 6 ÷ 100 = 1.2 kg
  • Soluble potash: 20 × 3 ÷ 100 = 0.6 kg

In other words, the numbers on the bag tell you the percentage of each nutrient by mass, so you can turn the label into actual kilograms of nutrients in the product.
